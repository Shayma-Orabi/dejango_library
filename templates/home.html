Library Policy for Adding Books and Publishers

To ensure the accuracy and consistency of our library catalog, employees must adhere to the following policies and guidelines when adding books and publishers to the database:

Book Addition Guidelines

  • Verify the book's details (e.g., title, author, version, year of publication, etc) against authoritative sources such as the publisher's website or official bibliographic databases before adding it to the system.
  • Assign the appropriate location based on the library's classification system.
  • Include any relevant metadata in the descrubtion, such as subject headings, keywords, or genres, to improve catalog accessibility.
  • Ensure the physical location of the book (e.g., Gener,section , shelf) is accurately recorded in the system.
  • Cross-check for duplicates in the catalog to avoid multiple entries of the same title or edition.

Publisher Addition Guidelines

  • Verify the authenticity of the publisher through trusted resources, ensuring accurate entry of the publisher's name, address, and contact information.
  • Use consistent naming conventions to avoid duplicate publisher entries (e.g., “Oxford University Press” instead of abbreviated or alternate forms).

Quality Assurance and Record Maintenance

  • Regularly review entries for accuracy and completeness, addressing any discrepancies or errors.
  • Report any issues or uncertainties to the library's catalog administrator or supervisor for resolution.
  • Follow established library protocols and software guidelines to maintain the integrity of the catalog.

Failure to comply with these policies may result in errors within the catalog and disrupt library operations. Employees are encouraged to seek assistance if they encounter challenges during the cataloging process.
